Seven ways AI is harming
our planet — and six kinder
choices you can make instead

No expertise required. Just care.

Scott Seivwright June 2026 ~8 min read Good AI
Share X LinkedIn Facebook WhatsApp

This is not a complicated article. It is an honest one. AI — the kind of computer programme that answers your questions, writes text, and generates pictures — is quietly doing real damage to the natural world. Most of the companies behind it would rather you did not know that. This article tells you what is happening, and then shows you six gentler options you can use instead. You do not need to be a tech expert. You just need to care.

Think of the internet as a vast forest of machines. Every time you use an AI tool — ask it a question, ask it to write something — somewhere in that forest, a huge room full of computers wakes up and starts working. Those computers need electricity. A great deal of it. And they get very hot, so they need water to cool them down. A great deal of that too.

Nobody tells you this when you click the button. That is part of the problem.

⚠️ The Seven Harms

Each of these is real. Each has been measured. Read them slowly.

Harm One

💧 It is drinking our fresh water

AI computers get extremely hot. To stop them overheating, data centres pump millions of litres of cold water through them — water drawn from rivers, reservoirs, and aquifers. Teaching one large AI system to work can use as much fresh water as a small town uses in a week. And this is happening in places where water is already scarce.

~700,000 litres of water used just to train one well-known AI system
Harm Two

🔥 Every question you ask burns fuel

Asking an AI a question uses about ten times more electricity than doing a normal internet search. That electricity mostly comes from power stations that burn gas or coal. Every day, billions of questions are asked. That is a very large fire, burning somewhere you cannot see.

10 times more electricity than a standard internet search, per query
Harm Three

🌍 The carbon debt is being left for our children

Building and running AI systems releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere — the same gas that is warming our climate. The AI industry is on track to produce as much carbon pollution as the entire global aviation industry within this decade. Unlike a return flight, no one is buying a carbon offset. The debt is invisible and growing.

Equal to aviation AI's projected carbon footprint by 2030
Harm Four

🪦 Mountains of broken equipment are piling up

The special computer chips needed to run AI go out of date very quickly — sometimes within a year or two. When they are thrown away, they become electronic waste. Much of this waste ends up in Ghana, India, and other countries, where it poisons soil and water with heavy metals. The people living near those waste sites never used the AI in the first place.

50 million tonnes of electronic waste produced globally each year — AI is speeding this up
Harm Five

⚡ Big companies are grabbing energy — at any cost

Microsoft, Google, and Amazon are so desperate for electricity to power their AI systems that they are buying up power stations that were due to close — and even building private nuclear reactors. Old coal and gas plants that should have been switched off are being switched back on. The planet pays the price so that tech giants can move faster.

3 nuclear reactors contracted by Microsoft in 2024, specifically for AI power
Harm Six

📈 Bigger is treated as better — always

The companies building AI have one goal: make it bigger, make it faster, get there first. They are not asking whether a smaller, more careful, more efficient approach might work just as well. Smaller AI systems — built with care rather than brute force — can do most tasks perfectly well, using a tiny fraction of the energy.

1,000 times the efficiency gap between a careful small model and a frontier giant
Harm Seven

🫥 You are being kept in the dark

No major AI company is required by law to tell you how much energy or water each of their products uses. The figures that do exist are buried in long annual reports, written in language designed to confuse rather than inform. You have no way of knowing, when you press 'generate', what environmental cost you have just incurred. That is not an accident. It is a deliberate choice.

No companies publish full environmental costs per individual AI query

🌿 Six Greener Choices — with links to try them

These tools exist. They work well. They are better for the planet. Each one has a link so you can visit them directly. You do not need to understand how they work — you just need to know they are there, and that choosing them matters.

⭐ Editor's Top Pick
Green Choice One

GreenPT

🇪🇺 Europe

GreenPT was built from the ground up with one purpose: to be a powerful AI tool that does not harm the planet. It runs entirely on renewable energy — not 'mostly' renewable, not 'offset' renewable, but genuinely 100% green electricity. It is hosted in Europe, which means your conversations are protected by strong European privacy laws.

What makes GreenPT remarkable is that it shows you, for every single question you ask, how much energy was used to answer it. You can see the environmental cost in real time. No other major AI tool does this. It is the most honest AI on this list.

✦ Use this instead of: ChatGPT or Google's AI for everyday questions, writing help, and research

Green Choice Two

Ecosia AI Search

🇩🇪 Germany (not-for-profit)

Ecosia is a search engine with a conscience — a not-for-profit company that plants trees with its profits and has invested over €18 million in renewable energy projects. Their AI search features run on renewable energy, and they publish their financial accounts openly every single month so you can see exactly where the money goes.

It is not just neutral — it actively does good. When you search with Ecosia, you are putting money into solar parks and reforestation.

✦ Use this instead of: Google Search and Google's AI Overview for everyday searching
Visit Ecosia →
#EcosiaAI#PlantTrees#NotForProfit#RenewableEnergy
Green Choice Three

Mistral AI

🇫🇷 France

Mistral is a French company building AI systems that are much smaller and more efficient than the American giants — and yet often just as capable for everyday tasks. Think of it like a well-tended allotment rather than an industrial farm: quieter, more careful, and surprisingly productive.

Their tools are openly published so anyone can inspect how they work. You can even run some of their smaller models on your own computer, completely offline.

✦ Use this instead of: ChatGPT-4 for writing, summarising, and answering questions
Visit Mistral AI →
#MistralAI#EfficientAI#OpenSource#EuropeanAI
Green Choice Four

Ollama — AI on your own computer

🌐 Open Source

Ollama is not an AI itself — it is a way of running AI on your own laptop or desktop computer, without sending your questions to a distant data centre at all. Your questions never leave your home. The only electricity used is what your own computer draws.

For anyone who values privacy and the land, this is as local and clean as AI can currently get. It does require some setting up, but there are friendly step-by-step guides on their website.

✦ Use this instead of: Any cloud-based AI, for private or everyday tasks at home
Visit Ollama →
#Ollama#LocalAI#PrivacyAI#ZeroCloudAI
Green Choice Five

Cohere

🇨🇦 Canada

Cohere is a Canadian company that powers its AI on renewable energy and has made a public commitment to reach net zero carbon by 2040 — with real steps along the way, not just a distant promise. They also offer free access to environmental charities and researchers through their For Good programme.

It is particularly well suited to reading and summarising long documents, reports, and articles.

✦ Use this instead of: OpenAI tools for reading long documents and writing assistance
Visit Cohere →
#CohereAI#NetZeroAI#AIForGood
Green Choice Six

Hugging Face — The Open Library

🌐 Open Source

Imagine a public library, but for AI tools — freely available, openly shared, and accountable. Hugging Face is a place where thousands of AI systems are published for anyone to inspect, use, or run on their own computer. Crucially, they actively measure and publish the carbon cost of building their tools.

In a world of corporate secrecy, that kind of transparency is a small act of courage. It also gives researchers and environmentalists the tools they need to hold the industry to account.

✦ Use this instead of: Proprietary AI tools, especially for research or specialist tasks
Visit Hugging Face →
#HuggingFace#OpenWeights#CarbonTransparency#OpenSourceAI

A Final Word

You do not need to understand how any of this works under the surface. You do not need a degree in computing or a young person's ease with technology.

What you need is what you already have: a sense that the earth matters. That water matters. That the choices we make — even small, quiet ones — carry weight.

The technology companies making billions from AI are counting on your confusion, your deference, your assumption that this is all beyond you. It is not beyond you.

Ask your AI where its power comes from. Choose the one that tells you honestly. Use less where you can. And share this with someone who still thinks none of this applies to them.

We are all just walking each other home. Let us not burn the forest down on the way.
