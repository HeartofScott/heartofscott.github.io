Britain's data centre queue is more than three times the size of the entire national grid

New research finds that applications to connect data centres to the British electricity system have reached 50 gigawatts — against a national grid operator forecast of just 9.9 to 14.6 GW needed by 2050. The Firewalkers calls for an immediate moratorium on greenfield applications.

Story angles

What makes this worth telling

01

The technologist who uses AI to protect the land from AI

A senior technology professional with an Oxford AI qualification and 30 years of digital transformation experience — who also happens to be a practising druid. The tension between those two identities became the platform.

02

The data centre land grab nobody voted for

Thousands of planning applications. A 12-week consultation window. Communities who don't know their rights. The AI infrastructure boom is the fastest land-grab in modern British history — and most people have no idea it is happening.

03

Faith and tech: an unexpected alliance

Druids, Muslims, Quakers, Hindus and indigenous land defenders — all arriving at the same question: who has the right to take the land? A movement that bridges digital technology and ancient spiritual traditions.

04

The Scottish campaign that could change everything

A data centre proposed for the Lammermuir Hills Special Landscape Area. A community organising to stop it. A 12-week window closing. The Firewalkers built the tools. Now we need the people.

05

Britain's phantom grid queue: the bubble in plain sight

50 gigawatts of data centre applications against a 2050 requirement of under 15 GW. Ofgem has already said the queue process isn't fit for purpose. The planning system hasn't caught up. Yet.

06

Good AI vs bad AI: drawing the line

Not all AI is the same. Some AI is efficient, renewable-powered, and genuinely serves the public interest. Some is being built at breakneck speed on greenfield land using billions of litres of water. The Firewalkers is building the framework to tell them apart.

Spokesperson biography

Scott Seivwright

Founder, The Firewalkers

Scott Seivwright

Edinburgh, Scotland · Available for interview

Scott Seivwright is a senior digital transformation leader with 30 years of experience working at the intersection of technology, organisational change, and environmental responsibility. He holds an Oxford AI Programme qualification, is co-founder of GreenPO.org, and is a member of the Green Software Foundation and Agilists for the Planet.

He is also a practising druid — OBOD Ovate, Elder and Gorse Druids Roslin, Awen Alba Edinburgh — with a deep commitment to Celtic spiritual traditions and land stewardship. The Firewalkers grew from the tension between these two parts of his life: a technologist who understood exactly how AI infrastructure worked, and a druid who felt what it cost the land.

Scott speaks with authority on AI infrastructure, data centre planning, environmental impact assessment, community organising, and responsible technology. He is direct, data-led, and capable of translating complex regulatory and technical arguments for general audiences. He is based in Edinburgh and available for remote and in-person interview.
