01 · Identity

Who We Are

The Firewalkers is a global movement using AI to protect the natural world from the environmental impact of data centre infrastructure. Founded in Scotland in June 2026. Credible, factual, urgent — rooted in land stewardship and driven by evidence.

02 · Colour

Accessibility rule: All text on coloured backgrounds must meet WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for large text). Amber on parchment passes. White on Forest passes. White on Slate passes. Never use Amber Bright as body text on white.

05 · Voice & Tone

How The Firewalkers Sounds

Authoritative and factual, grounded in data and evidence. Not a charity appeal. Not a protest slogan. A credible movement with a clear argument. The register is that of a well-sourced investigative reporter who also happens to love the land.

We areWe are not
Factual and evidencedAlarmist or catastrophising
Urgent but measuredSentimental or romantic
Direct and clearVague or corporate
Grounded in place and dataAbstract or ideological
Respectful of the reader's intelligenceLecturing or preachy
Specific — names, dates, placesGeneral — "the planet," "the future"
Avoid

"Our beautiful planet is under attack from the forces of greed and technology. We must stand together before it is too late for our children and their children."

Preferred

"A data centre has been proposed for the Lammermuir Hills — a Special Landscape Area. The consultation closes in 12 weeks. Most of the local community don't know it's happening."

Avoid

"Join us on this incredible journey as we fight for the earth and harness the power of community to make a difference!"

Preferred

"We watch planning applications. We translate them into plain language. We connect the people who know how to fight back and win."

Avoid

"AI is destroying everything sacred. The algorithms don't care about your forest or your hills. It's all profit and extraction."

Preferred

"The technology is not the enemy. How it is being built — at speed, in 12-week planning windows, on protected land — that is the problem."

Writing Rules

RuleDetail
Lead with the specificName the place, the date, the planning reference. Numbers before adjectives.
Active voice"A developer filed a planning application" not "A planning application was filed"
No superlativesAvoid "incredible," "amazing," "transformative" — let the facts do the work
Short sentences for urgencyOne fact per sentence when pace matters. Longer prose for context.
Headline structureStatement · Place · Consequence. "A Data Centre on the Lammermuirs. Twelve Weeks to Object."
Sign-off lineAlways end major pieces with: The future belongs to the stewards, not the extractors.
